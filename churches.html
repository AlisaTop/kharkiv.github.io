Cathedrals and Churches

Cult constructions of Kharkov

There are famous cathedrals and churches in Kharkov.

Annunciation Cathedral (Cathedral of the Annunciation of the Blessed Virgin Mary) This temple, a striking representative of the neo-Byzantine style of architecture, with an 80-meter bell tower, was built between 1889 and 1901 according to the project of the local architect Mikhail Lovtsov. Able to accommodate 4 thousand people. It is the Cathedral of the Kharkov Diocese of the Ukrainian Orthodox Church (Moscow Patriarchate). Patriarch Athanasius III and several holy bishops are buried in the Annunciation Cathedral. Where: Blagoveshchensk Square, 1

Annunciation Cathedral (Cathedral of the Annunciation of the Blessed Virgin Mary) Pokrovsky Cathedral (Cathedral of the Protection of the Holy Virgin) The oldest of all the existing Kharkov churches is Pokrovsky Cathedral, built in 1689 on the site of a wooden church, known since 1659. The Assumption Cathedral has existed since 1657, but it was completely destroyed and rebuilt in 1783. Where: Universitetskaya Street, 10/8 Where: Blagoveshchenskaya Square, 1

Church of the Holy Myrrh Bearing Women This temple is the youngest in Kharkov. Perhaps thanks to this, he got into today's Top. The Church of the Holy Myrrh-Bearing Women was created in the traditional Ukrainian style. The lower church has been working for a year already, and the entire square connecting Sumy and Chernyshevskaya streets has been landscaped by the opening of the entire complex. After a while, the painting of the temple will begin. An elevator is already working inside, which can be used by elderly people, people with disabilities and choristers: the lift will deliver parishioners from the lower church to the main prayer hall, and the choir to the balconies. Address: Kharkov, st. Chernyshevskaya, 17 (behind the Mirror Stream)
