Shevchenko's park

Nature & Parks in Kharkiv

Shevchenko Garden is a city park in the center of Kharkov. It is located in the historical Nagorny district and the Shevchenkovsky administrative district, between Sumskaya and Klochkovskaya streets and Freedom and Rymarskaya square.

Shevchenko Park is a real symbol of the city, its highlight. The history of the garden begins back in 1804, when the first trees were planted here by the founder of the Kharkov National University Vasily Nazarovich Karazin, in whose honor the university was named. The garden blended into the existing oak grove, which was then located on the outskirts of the city. The upper part became a landscape park, and the lower part became a botanical garden. The first name given to the park by the founding fathers is Universitetskiy.

  • The garden was laid in 1804-1805
  • In the 30s of the XX century, the park was reconstructed
  • During World War II, the park was badly damaged. About 60% of pre-war green spaces were destroyed
  • The total height of the monument is 16.5 meters
